Confederate Secession

The first state to secede from the Union was South Carolina, on December 20, 1860. The state did so because citizens were worried that a Republican President would make efforts to abolish slavery, which was not wanted by the South. A total of 10 other states seceded from the Union. The first 6 were part of the deep south, with the most slaves. They were, in order: Mississippi, Florida, Alabama, Georgia, Louisiana, and Texas. Adding South Carolina, which was first, made 7 states that were part of the deep south. Afterward, another 4 states seceded. They were, in order: Virginia, Arkansas, North Carolina, Tennessee. In the end, a total of 11 states seceded from the union, beginning with South Carolina on December 20, 1860 1860 and ending with Tennessee on June 8, 1861.
