Confederate Secession

The first state to secede from the Union was South Carolina. The state did so because citizens were worried that a Republican President would make efforts to abolish slavery, which was not wanted by the South. A total of 10 other states seceded from the Union, in order: Mississippi, Florida, Alabama, Georgia, Louisiana
