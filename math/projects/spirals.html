Parametric Spirals

Edmund Lam

We start a parametric spiral first by visualizing how a spiral works. We can visualize making our spiral just as how we would draw it, by starting from the center and spiraling outwards, like so:



We see that for every t in the parametric equation, our spiral will turn one degree and also get one unit further from the center. This is important because it means that our spiral will be on a circle with radius t at degree t from the center.



Now, the rest is only to put our idea into math. We know that the degree measure of our point, and that it is on a circle, so we use the fact that given the radian of our point on a circle, the (x,y) point is equal to (rcos(d),rsin(d)) if r is the radius and d is the direction. Now, since we also know that the degree and radius are equal, we get our equation, (tcos(t),tsin(t)).
